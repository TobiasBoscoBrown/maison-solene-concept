A quiet luxury

Built for the people who notice the small things.

Maison Solène sits where the boreal forest meets the widening river, an hour past the last traffic light. Inside, fourteen rooms in warm oak, linen, and blackened steel. Outside, a thousand acres of spruce, a private stretch of shoreline, and a sky with nothing in the way of it.

We keep the place small on purpose. No queue at the desk, no script at the door — a host who knows your name and a fire that is already lit.

La Table

Dinner is whatever the valley gave us today.

One menu, written each afternoon. Trout from the river, vegetables from the garden behind the kitchen, bread pulled from the oven while you settle in. Twenty-eight seats, one long fire, and a cellar that leans hard toward Québec growers.

Served nightly, 6 to 9. Open to the public by reservation.

Nordic Baths

Hot, cold, river, repeat.

A thermal circuit cut into the slope above the water — wood-fired sauna, cold plunge fed from the spring, and three open-air pools that steam against the trees. Reserve a private session at dusk and stay until the stars arrive.

Complimentary for overnight guests. Robes and tea provided.

Experiences

No light pollution for miles. We leave blankets and a star chart out; the rest is up to the weather.
